Johnson & Johnson (NYSE:JNJ) reported strong Q1 2026 performance with operational sales growth of 6.4%, exceeding consensus expectations. The company has a robust portfolio pipeline with 28 platforms or products generating at least $1 billion in annual revenue, aiming for $100 billion in annual revenue by 2026. Innovative Medicine segment delivered operational sales growth of 7.4%, with 10 brands growing double digits, highlighting strong performance in oncology and immunology. MedTech segment reported Q1 operational sales growth of 4.6%, driven by growth in cardiovascular, surgery, and vision sectors. JNJ increased its operational sales guidance for 2026 to a range of 5.9% to 6.9%, with a midpoint of $100.2 billion, reflecting confidence in sustained growth. Net earnings for the quarter were $5.2 billion, with a decrease in adjusted net earnings and diluted earnings per share compared to Q1 2025. STELARA faced a significant decline of 61.7% due to biosimilar competition, impacting overall sales growth. Cost of goods sold deleveraged by 10 basis points, driven by tariffs and unfavorable mix in the Innovative Medicine business. Selling, marketing, and administrative expenses increased by 180 basis points due to heavier investment in new launches and acquisitions. MedTech margin declined from 25.9% to 22.3%, primarily due to tariffs and unfavorable onetime items recorded in 2025. Q: Can you remind us of how you're positioning ICOTYDE in the market now that you have full details on the label and pricing? Also, how should we think about the ramp of reimbursement coverage and any sampling plans? A: Jennifer Taubert, Executive Vice President, Worldwide Chairman, Pharmaceuticals, explained that ICOTYDE is off to a fast start with strong early enthusiasm from physicians and patients. It is positioned as the first and only targeted oral peptide that blocks the IL-23 receptor, offering complete skin clearance and favorable safety. The company is in positive conversations with payers to drive early and broad access. ICOTYDE is expected to become the preferred first-line systemic therapy, complementing TREMFYA as the first choice biologic treatment. Q: What are you seeing in your end markets for MedTech, and how are you thinking about the remainder of the year? A: Tim Schmid, Executive Vice President, Worldwide Chairman of MedTech, stated that the strategy focused on higher growth and innovation markets is working. Q1 unfolded as expected, with 4.6% operational growth, despite being a seasonally quieter quarter. The company is confident in further acceleration throughout 2026, with growth catalysts in Cardiovascular, Vision, and Surgery. Q: How do you see the potential of ICOTYDE compared to TREMFYA, and what is the pathway to becoming one of the company's largest products? A: Joaquin Duato, Chairman of the Board, Chief Executive Officer, emphasized that ICOTYDE has significant potential in psoriatic arthritis and IBD, similar to TREMFYA. The product is expected to expand the market by offering a convenient oral option for patients who are not on biologics. The company sees ICOTYDE as a major growth driver, contributing to double-digit growth by the end of the decade. Q: Could you provide insights into the commercialization plan and rollout for INLEXZO? A: Jennifer Taubert highlighted that INLEXZO is outperforming recent launches in non-muscle invasive bladder cancer. The product fits seamlessly into urology practice, and following the J-code approval, there has been a significant increase in new patient insertions. The company is excited about expanding into broader populations and leveraging its MedTech capabilities for training and support. Q: What are your views on the US surgical and contact lens market, particularly regarding the decline in US Surgical in the quarter? A: Tim Schmid noted that Vision delivered solid growth, with contact lenses driven by the ACUVUE OASYS 1-day family. Surgical Vision growth was offset in the US due to competitive pressures, but the company remains confident in its clinical position with TECNIS Odyssey and the upcoming launch of TECNIS PureSee.
