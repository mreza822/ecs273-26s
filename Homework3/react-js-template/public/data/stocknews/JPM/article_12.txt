Here's why. Goldman Sachs delivered a warning to laid-off tech workers that it will take more time to find a new job than expected This article first appeared on GuruFocus. JPMorgan Chase & Co. (NYSE:JPM) is moving back into the bond market with plans to raise at least $7 billion through an investment-grade offering, according to people familiar with the matter, coming shortly after the bank reported a record quarterly trading performance. The deal is expected to be structured in as many as four parts, with maturities ranging from four to 11 years, including a longest-dated bond due in 2037 that is being marketed at roughly 1.1 percentage points above Treasuries. Proceeds are intended for general corporate purposes, and the timing appears to coincide with improving market conditions, where easing borrowing costslinked in part to renewed optimism around a potential Middle East peace dealhave encouraged issuers to return to the primary market. Across the broader market, issuance activity is picking up quickly, with around eight firms potentially pricing high-grade bond deals on Wednesday, according to a Bloomberg poll of syndicate underwriters. That follows $10.9 billion of bonds priced on Tuesday and additional overnight issuance from four foreign firms, pointing to a reopening in global credit markets. Goldman Sachs Group Inc. (GS) helped set the tone earlier in the week, pricing $6.5 billion in notes on Monday, and major banks are expected to account for a significant portion of a projected $40 billion in investment-grade issuance. If that volume is reached, it would mark the busiest week in over a month, suggesting issuers could be accelerating funding plans while market windows remain supportive. For JPMorgan, the return to debt markets comes alongside a mixed operating backdrop. Trading revenue rose 20% year over year in the first quarter, with both equities and fixed income, currencies and commodities businesses outperforming expectations. At the same time, the bank reversed a prior increase to its projected 2026 net interest income, which could indicate a more cautious outlook on lending profitability. The firm has already been active in debt issuance this year, raising $9 billion in January across two deals, including a $6 billion transaction immediately following its fourth-quarter results, and this latest move could reflect a broader effort by large financial institutions to secure funding while conditions remain favorable.
