To own Halliburton, you generally need to believe its international and technology-led oilfield services can offset soft spots in North American shale and support earnings growth. The YPF Vaca Muerta win reinforces the near term catalyst of expanding higher value international work, while partially addressing the key risk of overdependence on North America. It also adds credibility to Halliburton’s lower carbon, digital frac offering, but does not eliminate long term decarbonization and regulatory headwinds. The recent Strategic Collaboration Agreement with PETRONAS Suriname and Valaris lines up closely with the YPF news. Both point to Halliburton pushing bundled, technology rich services in emerging and offshore basins, which is central to the bullish catalyst that international projects and automation can broaden its revenue base. Together, these developments strengthen the case that Halliburton’s growth story is increasingly tied to complex, higher tech work outside North America.
