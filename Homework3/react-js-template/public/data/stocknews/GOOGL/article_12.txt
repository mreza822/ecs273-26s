Google (NASDAQ:GOOG) is back in the legal spotlight after a smaller rival accused it of tightening its grip on the Android app ecosystem. The lawsuit comes from Aptoide, which claims Google is using its control over Android and Google Play to block competing app stores from gaining real traction. Filed in a San Francisco federal court and first reported by Reuters, the case is seeking an injunction along with triple damages. Aptoide argues that even though it offers lower commissions and cheaper distribution for developers, it still cannot compete on a level playing field. Warning! What makes this more than just a small player complaint is the scale Aptoide is pointing to. The company says it already hosts around 436,000 apps and serves over 200 million users annually, positioning itself as the third largest Android app store. It has also been raising similar concerns for years, including a complaint with EU regulators back in 2014. That history adds weight as Alphabet continues to deal with broader antitrust scrutiny.
