A Discounted Cash Flow, or DCF, model estimates what a company might be worth today by projecting its future cash flows and discounting them back to a present value. It is essentially asking what Chevron's future cash generation could be worth in today's dollars. For Chevron, the model uses a 2 Stage Free Cash Flow to Equity approach. The latest twelve month free cash flow is about US$18.0b. Analysts have provided explicit free cash flow estimates for several years ahead, and Simply Wall St extends those projections further, with free cash flow for 2030 estimated at US$30.3b. Each of these future cash flows is discounted back to the present using a required rate of return to account for time and risk. Putting those projections together, the model arrives at an estimated intrinsic value of US$375.99 per share. Compared with the recent share price of US$187.02, the DCF output implies Chevron trades at roughly a 50.3% discount to this intrinsic estimate, which suggests the stock may be undervalued on this cash flow view. For profitable companies, the P/E ratio is a useful way to think about what you are paying for each dollar of current earnings. It links the share price directly to earnings, which many investors watch closely when weighing how much they are prepared to pay for a business like Chevron. What counts as a "normal" or "fair" P/E often reflects how the market views a company’s growth potential and risk. Higher expected growth or lower perceived risk can justify a higher multiple, while slower growth or higher uncertainty can point to a lower one. Chevron currently trades on a P/E of 30.29x. This sits above the Oil and Gas industry average of 15.01x and above the peer group average of 23.69x. Simply Wall St’s Fair Ratio framework estimates a P/E of 31.03x for Chevron, based on factors such as its earnings profile, industry, profit margins, size and risk characteristics. This Fair Ratio aims to be more tailored than simple peer or industry comparisons because it accounts for multiple drivers of what investors might reasonably pay for Chevron’s earnings. Comparing the current P/E of 30.29x with the Fair Ratio of 31.03x suggests the shares are about in line with this earnings based view of value.
